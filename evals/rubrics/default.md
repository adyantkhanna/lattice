# Lattice Research Agent Evaluation Rubric

Score each criterion 0–5. Maximum total: 25 points.

---

## Accuracy (0–5)
- **5** All factual claims are correct and consistent with authoritative sources
- **4** Minor inaccuracies (e.g. approximate numbers) that do not affect conclusions
- **3** Some notable inaccuracies but the core answer is directionally correct
- **2** Significant inaccuracies that would mislead the reader
- **1** Mostly incorrect
- **0** Completely wrong or hallucinates with confidence

## Citation Quality (0–5)
- **5** Every non-trivial claim carries a working inline link to a relevant source
- **4** Most claims cited; all links functional and go to the right content
- **3** Some claims cited; most links functional
- **2** Few citations, or many broken/irrelevant links
- **1** Citations present but not substantive (e.g., links to homepages only)
- **0** No citations

## Source Relevance (0–5)
- **5** All cited sources are directly relevant and authoritative (primary papers, official docs, high-quality technical blogs)
- **4** Mostly relevant; one or two tangential sources
- **3** Mix of relevant and tangential
- **2** Mostly tangential
- **1** Sources don't match the topic
- **0** No sources or completely irrelevant

## Specificity (0–5)
- **5** Concrete numbers, benchmarks, mechanisms, or examples throughout; no vague generalities
- **4** Specific with minor vague passages
- **3** Mix of specific and general
- **2** Mostly general statements
- **1** Very generic; no concrete details
- **0** Completely vague

## Level Calibration (0–5)
- **5** Depth and terminology perfectly matched to the question; neither oversimplified nor unnecessarily dense
- **4** Slight mismatch (marginally too basic or too advanced)
- **3** Noticeable mismatch
- **2** Significant mismatch
- **1** Completely wrong level for the audience
- **0** Incomprehensible
